Primary Insurance Amount (PIA): The base monthly Social Security payout, proportional to the person's pre-retirement monthly income.
Full Retirement Age (FRA): The age at which the full PIA is earned. This age is 67 for anyone born after 1960. Filing before FRA reduces PIA while filing after increases it.
Spousal benefit: A person may raise their Social Security payout up to half of their spouse's PIA. Filing before FRA reduces this benefit.
Survivor benefit: A person may raise their Social Security payout up to 100% of their deceased spouse's payout.
